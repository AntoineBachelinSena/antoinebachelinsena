About

Antoine Bachelin Sena is a French strategic risk analyst based in São Paulo since 2011. He specialises in transnational hybrid networks, illicit financial flows, and institutional vulnerabilities across Latin America, with particular focus on the Guiana Shield and European outermost regions.

Combining fifteen years of direct field experience with a French perspective on sovereignty and state resilience, his work examines how criminal and hybrid actors exploit jurisdictional asymmetries between strict European Union law and more permissive neighbouring environments.

He is the Founding President & Director of Strategy of the Institut Atlantique Sud, currently developing in-depth analysis on French Guiana as a laboratory of Europe’s strategic vulnerabilities in the twenty-first century.
